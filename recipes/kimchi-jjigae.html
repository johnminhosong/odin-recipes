Kimchi Jjigae (김치찌개)

Description

Embark on a flavorful journey with Kimchi Jjigae, a beloved Korean stew that harmonizes spicy, tangy, and savory notes to create a soul-warming culinary experience. Bursting with the vibrant essence of fermented kimchi, this hearty stew is a celebration of bold flavors and comfort. Enjoy!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound kimchi, chopped into bite sized pieces
  • 1/4 cup kimchi brine
  • 1/2 pound pork shoulder (or pork belly)
  • 1/2 package tofu, sliced into 1/2 inch thick bite size pieces
  • 3 green onions
  • 1 medium onion, sliced (1 cup)
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons gochugaru
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang
  • 1 teaspoon toasted sesame oil
  • 7 large dried anchovies, heads and guts removed
  • 1/3 cup Korean radish, sliced thinly
  • 4x5 inch dried kelp
  • 4 cups water

Steps

  1. Make anchovy stock: Put anchovies, daikon, green onion roots (from the 3 green onions), and dried kelp in a sauce pan. Add 4 cups of water and boil for 20 minutes over medium high heat. Lower heat to low for 5 more minutes then strain ingredients.
  2. Combine ingredients: In a separate pot, place kimchi, kimchi brine, pork, and onion. Slice 2 green onions and add to the pot. Add salt, sugar, gochujang, gochugaru. Once all ingredients are in, drizzle sesame oil over top and add the anchovy stock. Cover with lid and cook for 10 minutes over medium high heat.
  3. Add tofu: Open and mix in the seasonings with a spoon. Lay the sliced tofu over the top. Cover and cook for another 10 to 15 minutes over medium heat.
  4. Serve: Garnish with chopped green onions. Remove from the heat and serve with rice and other side dishes. Enjoy!
